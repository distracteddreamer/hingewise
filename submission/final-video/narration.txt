AI tutors are excellent at giving answers. But a perfect explanation can leave the learner's wrong mental model completely intact. Hingewise starts somewhere else: explain what you think is happening.

Here, a learner believes summer happens because Earth moves closer to the Sun. Hingewise sends that teach-back to GPT-5.6, not to grade it, but to reconstruct the causal reasoning behind it.

It preserves the sound footing: the Sun supplies energy, and distance can affect intensity. Then it locates the earliest wrong turn: assuming Earth moves dramatically closer during one hemisphere's summer. Everything after that is a consequence.

Now for the distinctive part. Flip that one hinge and the downstream reasoning realigns around Earth's tilt, the angle of sunlight, and the length of each day. The interface makes the minimum conceptual change visible instead of hiding it inside a chat response.

Hingewise also explains why the original belief felt reasonable. Then it asks one prediction before revealing the fix: if Earth is one distance from the Sun in July, why can Canada be in summer while Argentina is in winter? That contrast repairs the model through active reasoning, not memorization.

Finally, a transfer question checks the new model: if Earth's orbit became perfectly circular, would seasons disappear? One misconception, one repair, and one proof that it transferred.
